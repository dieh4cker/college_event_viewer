Class Tests

Class Tests are internal assessments conducted by the institute to evaluate students' academic progress during the semester. These tests help students understand their preparation level and encourage regular study habits throughout the course. Two Class Tests are conducted in every semester.

Class Test I:

Class Test I is conducted during the early phase of the semester. It evaluates the understanding of the initial topics taught in the course.
Maximum Marks: 30
Syllabus Coverage: First two chapters of each subject
Purpose: To assess the foundational understanding of the subject and help students identify areas for improvement.

Class Test II:

Class Test II is conducted after the completion of the next portion of the syllabus.
Maximum Marks: 30
Test Duration: 1 Hour
Syllabus Coverage: Next two chapters of each subject

House Test

House Tests are internal examinations conducted by the institute before the final semester examinations. These tests help students prepare effectively for their final exams by evaluating their understanding of most of the syllabus covered during the semester. House Tests are designed to simulate the pattern and environment of the final examinations so that students can assess their readiness and improve their performance.

House Test Examination:

The House Test is conducted after the majority of the course syllabus has been completed. It helps students revise their subjects and identify areas that require additional preparation before the final examination.
Maximum Marks: 60
Test Duration: 3 Hours
Syllabus Coverage: Approximately 80% of the total syllabus of each subject
Purpose: To prepare students for the final examination and evaluate their overall understanding of the course.

Final Examination

Final Examinations are the main academic assessments conducted at the end of every semester. These examinations evaluate the overall understanding and knowledge gained by students throughout the entire semester. The Final Examination is conducted according to the academic schedule provided by the examination authority. It is the most important assessment that determines the final performance of students in each subject.

Final Examination Details:

The Final Examination is conducted after the completion of the full course syllabus.
Maximum Marks: 60
Examination Duration: 3 Hours
Syllabus Coverage: Full syllabus of each subject
Purpose: To evaluate the complete academic performance of students during the semester.
